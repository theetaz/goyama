# Code of Conduct

CropDoc is committed to being a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free community. This applies to every space the project operates in — the repository, issues, PRs, discussions, chat channels, events.

## Our standards

**Expected behavior**

- Be respectful and considerate. Disagree on ideas, not on people.
- Assume good faith. Ask for clarification before assigning intent.
- Share credit. Agriculture is a field built on generations of collective knowledge; treat sources and contributors accordingly.
- Welcome newcomers. A patient answer now saves a frustrated one later.
- Prefer writing that works across languages and literacy levels.

**Unacceptable behavior**

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks based on ethnicity, religion, caste, gender, sexual orientation, nationality, age, disability, or socioeconomic status.
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing).
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or reviews.
- Political or religious campaigning within project spaces.
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — online and offline — and when an individual is representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Report violations by emailing the maintainers (address to be published in the repo once available) or opening a confidential issue. Reports are handled promptly and confidentially. Possible actions range from a private warning to a permanent ban from the project.

We will not retaliate against good-faith reporters.
